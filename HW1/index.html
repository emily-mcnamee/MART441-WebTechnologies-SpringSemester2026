Memory of a Game

One of my most prominent game memories was playing The Legend of Zelda: The WindWaker for the Wii U as a pre-teen. The game's map is a vast ocean, as its explained at the beginning that the Gods flooded the land of Hyrule to seal the King of Demons away. You have to usually sail for in-game hours between islands; finding treasure and fighting oceaneanic monsters along the trip. The sailing time between islands leaves you a while to contemplate your next plan and to take in the sights.

During one of these long-haul sailing trips to get to an island on the other side of the map, I remember wondering why the world was so sparsely populated; save for a few main islands, the world was almost meloncholichally empty of friendly life. Then, a realization that was far too sobering for a 10-13 year old hit me; the reason that the human population was so small was because they were the descendants of the few people that survived the apolocyliptic flood.

While the meta reason is probably because there simply wasn't a good reason for the game developers to try to make more than a few human NPCs, wether intentional or not, it was an unforgettable reading of environmental storytelling that stayed with me, even now.


"The Legend of Zelda: The WindWaker HD (2013). Nintendo"
